3484 Magazine

Creative Director

3484 Magazine is a semesterly news magazine at Georgia Tech that focuses on long term issues in Atlanta. I co-founded the magazine in my freshman year at Tech, establishing style guides, color palettes, and guidelines for articles. The magazine is print and digital. Sam's Neat Projects

Creator

I started Sam's Neat Projects because of a lack of easy-to-understand Arduino tutorial videos online. Eventually it evolved from Arduino tutorials to tutorials on STEM projects for kids. My goal was to create tutorials that were aesthetically pleasing, easy to understand, and short. I combined an overhead camera rig with stop motion photography to create a unique DIY style.

Carpool Trip Visualization

I created a visualization of a carpool project using Carto.VL. A series of rings shows the number of passengers in the car, and blue dots show pickups. All of these vehicles are driving to the same block in Midtown Atlanta.

On Demand System Animation

Chinchilla Music Video

I was asked to create a music video with interesting visuals. The song was about a stalker, so I decided to style it similar to a ransom note. I used a combination of actual stop-motion photography and faux stop-motion photography by animating images in Final Cut Pro.

Disconnected

This article explores the ways that highways in Atlanta were used as a tool for displacement and segregation. I styled headers and statistics to look like highway signs. The signs were created using Adobe XD and Photoshop. On the third page, I combined present aerial imagery with archival aerial imagery from 1949 to show the destruction caused by the interstate system.

Changing City

This was an infographic from the Fall 2020 issue of 3484 Magazine. My goal was to showcase new transit developments, trails and parks around Atlanta. I created icons for each development to give a uniform and understandable visual explanation for what each development would look like.
