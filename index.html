About Me

(Sam)
I'm a computer science student graduating from Georgia Tech in May 2023, with experience in design, editorial photo illustration, animation, data visualization, video, and web development.
Data Visualization and Presentations Intern

I worked with the digital storytelling team at The Atlanta Journal-Constitution. I created data visualizations, social media graphics and animations, wrote an article, and created a style guide and assets for a series of articles on climate change.

3484 Magazine

Creative Director

3484 Magazine is a semesterly news magazine at Georgia Tech that focuses on long term issues in Atlanta. I co-founded the magazine in my freshman year at Tech, and was the creative director throughout college, establishing style guides, color palettes, and guidelines for articles. The magazine is print and digital.

Sam's Neat Projects

Creator

I started Sam's Neat Projects because of a lack of easy-to-understand Arduino tutorial videos online. Eventually it evolved from Arduino tutorials to tutorials on STEM projects for kids. Marta Map

I created a map to show a bus rapid transit route in Atlanta. The paths were originally polyline files that I added to a map in Mapbox Studio, but I converted them to vectors and combined them with aerial imagery in the Adobe Suite. I wanted to use aerial imagery because our Instagram posts are usually pretty reliant on vector graphics, so I always try to add in actual photographs when I can. Sometimes aerial imagery can be confusing since most people don't use satellite maps in everyday life and aren't familiar with an overhead view of their city. However, this map worked out because of the number of distinct landmarks such as stadiums and Downtown Atlanta's unique grid. If I were to make this map again, I would either use a more contrasting color for the vectors or add a subtle shadow to help separate the vector graphics from the background map. Marietta Street Time Lapse

Whenever I go on the roof of my apartment, I'm constantly looking at something new since there's always so much cool stuff going on. It was too windy for a traditional time-lapse this night, so I tried to recreate that feeling in this video by using a tripod and manually taking exposures of different lengths at irregular intervals to add a more organic feel. This was something I made for fun one night, but I would love to recreate this on a larger scale for a music video or promotional video one day.

Fridge Town

This is a fun personal project I started in my apartment. I don't think it's applicable to any jobs (even though I did once work at a refrigerator startup), but I had a lot of fun with it and wanted to share. I drew buildings and roads onto isometric patterns that I printed onto magnet sheets, which allowed all the buildings to be easily connected with the same scale and perspective. After drawing a building I would cut it out and put it on our fridge. I documented each new addition, and even got friends and roommates to add to it too. Once I had enough pictures I turned it into a gif to show its progress over time. I documented the process in a thread on Twitter.
